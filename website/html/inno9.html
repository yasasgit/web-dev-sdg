Sri Lanka Navy develops remote-controlled smart appliance to test and treat COVID-19 patients



Apr 09, Colombo: Sri Lanka Navy has developed a remote-controlled smart appliance called ‘Medi Mate’ in a bid to treat and test COVID-19 patients from a safe distance.
The smart appliance developed by the Navy’s Research and Development Unit based on a basic concept proposed by Thushara Kelum Wadasinghe, an inventor will allow healthcare workers to remain at a safe distance from the highly infectious patients.
The Navy handed over the robotic appliance for use at the Dr. Neville Fernando Teaching Hospital Wednesday.
This smart appliance will also help healthcare workers to diagnose ailments, communicate with patients and dispense medicine and meals to them, without getting closer to the infected people.
The remote-controlled device also has an automated nozzle which sprays a stream of sanitizing liquid to the hands of the patient, allowing him/her to sanitize the hands before touching any equipment or drugs placed on the device.
As such, this innovative appliance will help protect doctors and healthcare workers from the highly contagious novel coronavirus, when they are dealing with COVID-19 patients.
The Research and Development Unit of Sri Lanka Navy has also developed a disinfection chamber recently and similar chambers, with added features, have been set up at Kalubowila Teaching Hospital, Neville Fernando Teaching Hospital and University Hospital KDU.
